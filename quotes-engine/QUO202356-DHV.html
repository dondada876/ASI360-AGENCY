Section 1

Executive Summary

Problem Statement

The access control programming workstation has gone missing. Without it, no one can add, change, or revoke resident credentials on the existing Keri system — leaving the property unable to manage access for 396 units across 15 buildings.

Community Scale: Diamond Heights Village comprises 396 units across 15 buildings, serving an estimated 700–800 residents. The scope of the credential migration is proportional to that scale.

Root Cause Analysis — Two Failures, Not One

The hardware is fine. Green LEDs are active on every controller. Wiring is intact. This is not a system failure — it is a data management failure caused by two compounding issues:

IssueWhat HappenedWhat It Cost
1. No Backup ProtocolThe credential database and Doors.NET configuration were stored only on one physical machine with no backupAll 900 resident credentials lost when workstation went missing
2. Missing WorkstationThe sole programming station was misplacedNo one can add, change, or revoke access credentials
The Preventable Scenario

With a weekly automated backup in place, the recovery cost would have been approximately $750 in hardware + 3 hours of labor ≈ $1,000 total — system back online the same day. The absence of any backup turned a routine hardware replacement into a multi-week credential management crisis affecting 396 units across 15 buildings (~700–800 residents). ASI 360 will implement automated weekly backup as part of this engagement. If a workstation is ever lost or damaged again, recovery is a single afternoon.

Our Approach — Field Intelligence First

Because ASI 360 conducted a full field intelligence brief before drafting this proposal, we can state with confidence: there is no reason to replace all 10 door readers. Eight of 10 are functioning. Replacing them would be a misallocation of capital — roughly $4,000–$6,000 in unnecessary hardware and labor. We identified the 2 specific readers at weatherized main-entry positions that are swappable and that unlock mobile + keypad capability for the whole system.

On Keri Borealis cloud: we recommend it, but with transparency — it is a per-door license that gets expensive at full-campus scale. For this property, applying Borealis to 2 doors (the new readers) gives you cloud management where it matters most while keeping annual costs low. The hybrid approach — workstation for the 8 retained doors, Borealis for the 2 upgraded ones — is the right balance for 2026.

Why ASI 360 Recommends the CSR-6.2L for Diamond Heights

DHV's two main-entry doors have mullion-mount frames — the CSR-6.2L is a direct mechanical drop-in, no new rough-in or wall-box work required. The CSR-6.4L offers the same capabilities but requires single-gang boxes, which DHV's frames do not currently support without additional labor.
